VARIETIES

Alphonso

They are medium-sized with golden sunshine-yellow skin. Ratnagiri Alphonso mangoes are the most famous in India and are highly consumed mangoes. It is primarily cultivated in Maharashtra, particularly in the Ratnagiri, Devgad, and Konkan regions.

Kesar

Kesar mangoes are also known as kesari because of its saffron (kesar in Hindi) colour. It is known for its unique sweet taste and frequent and distinctive flavour. It is the second most popular mango in India. They are also medium-sized with an oval shape

Dasheri

Dasheri Mango are well known for their sweetness and more pulp. It is a medium to large-sized mango with small seeds. It is a juicy variety of mango with thin, yellow skin with light red or orange spots.

Totapuri

Totapuri Mangoes are also known as Banganapalli, Bangalore or Sandersha mangoes. It is named tota puri because when it is ripped it has a green colour with a parrotâ€™s beak appearance. Totapuri mangoes have a unique taste that is less sweet as compared to Alphonso and Kesar.

Pairi

Straight from the coastal state of Maharashtra comes the Pairi mango, a small but mighty fruit. With its distinct sweet and sour taste and firm, fiberless flesh, the Pairi mango is perfect for devouring right off the seed.

Langra

Langra mango is very popular among the Indians. It originated from Varanasi, Uttar Pradesh. The reason behind its unique name is- that it was first found on the farm of a man without legs, and then langra mango became famous with its name.
